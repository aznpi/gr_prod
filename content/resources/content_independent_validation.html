KPMG Report on Global Relay’s Business, Operational and Security Controls

KPMG conducts annual independent testing and validation on our business, operational, and security controls. We are proud of this extensive testing and the perfect results we have achieved year after year, with no exceptions. This comprehensive third party validation is unique in the archiving industry and represents an ongoing investment in our customers and partners.

Specifically, the KPMG Report provides assurances of and transparency into the high standards of our internal controls related to:

  1. Physical Security: Physical safeguards at our headquarters and data centers.
  2. Change Management: Frameworks for guiding software development releases, operations, and change control.
  3. Network Security and Availability: System architecture, redundancy, access, and security.
  4. Message Processing: Inbound message processing, secure storage, data center replication, and end-user access.
  5. Data Import, Export, and Deletion: Policies, procedures, and methodologies for securely handling customer data.
  6. Security Policies and Standards: Policies and standards governing privacy and confidentiality.
  7. Personnel Policies and Procedures: Employee lifecycle management.
  8. SAML Based Authentication: Verification of the security and correctness of our SAML authentication service.
  9. Software Security Testing: Automated and manual software testing for security vulnerabilities.

Service Organization Control (SOC) 2 Audits

SOC 2 audits test and report on the design and operating effectiveness of non-financial internal controls at cloud vendors and other organizations that perform outsourced services for their customers. These audits are based on Trust Service Principles that cover policies, communications, procedures, and monitoring in five areas.

Global Relay’s two mirrored data centers undergo SOC 2 Type 2 audits at least annually. The resulting SOC2 reports address the following Trust Service Principles:

  • Security: The system is protected against unauthorized access (both physical and logical).
  • Availability: The system is available for operation and use as committed or agreed.

Independent Penetration Testing

KPMG conducts annual independent security penetration testing (“ethical hacking”) on Global Relay’s internet-facing systems and applications. This testing simulates access attempts by unauthenticated individuals to identify, validate, and attempt to exploit vulnerability that might be used by attack agents (e.g., malicious persons on the internet, malicious internal individuals, and cyber criminal organizations).

We are very proud that KPMG’s penetration testing has never identified any significant security vulnerabilities.
