Free Air & Evaporative Cooling

Servers generate massive amounts of heat. Air conditioning to cool these servers is by far the largest use of power in traditional data centers. Instead of air conditioning, we use free air and evaporative cooling.

Free Air Cooling: For most of the year, the data center is kept cool by pulling in fresh outside air and circulating it through the building – a process known as ‘free air cooling.’ The location of our data center, with its mild winters and pleasantly warm summers, allows us to use free air cooling during all but the highest peaks in temperatures, which typically occur less than two weeks per year.

Evaporative Cooling: During temperature peaks, we use ‘evaporative cooling,’ a highly reliable, simple system that uses water – not electricity. The data center uses fans to pull up to 150,000 cubic feet of air (CFM) per minute into the building. This air is run through water blankets that boost the humidity and drop the temperature – basic science and free air-conditioning!

Power Savings

During normal operations, the data center is powered by hydroelectricity – a power source that does not create greenhouse gases. In addition the design of our data center cuts electricity usage by 50% when compared to traditional data center designs. We save power through:

  • Free air and evaporative cooling – no air conditioning required
  • European standard 400V for power distribution, main breakers, and IT equipment, which is more efficient than the 230V North American standard
  • Variable frequency drives that ensure fans, motors, and pumps work only as hard as they need to
  • LED lighting and occupancy sensors

Uninterruptable Power Supply (UPS) System

If the data center’s primary utility power fails, our backup generators pick up the electrical load. However, the generators require approximately 6-8 seconds to start.

To prevent downtime during this gap, we use a flywheel UPS system. A flywheel is a rotor that constantly spins around a bearing at 7700 RPM (picture a 660 lb steel donut spinning at very high speed). The flywheels store kinetic energy (energy generated by motion) that is converted into electrical energy. This electrical energy powers the servers during the time it takes for the generators to start.

By using flywheel UPS, we have eliminated the toxic, failure-prone lead-acid battery UPS systems used in most data centers which must be replaced every three years.

Systems and Controls

Overview

Global Relay services run on true enterprise systems, servers, and storage architecture with clustered, fully redundant configurations and automatic failovers, eliminating or minimizing single points of failure. In order to address compliance, availability, and business continuity needs, we host our private cloud in two mirrored SOC-audited data centers separated by more than 2,500 miles. In the unlikely event of a disaster or other significant business disruption, services fail over to the secondary data center to ensure extremely high uptime.

Network

We route network traffic between two redundant Tier 1 ISPs providing 10G connectivity. Our two mirrored data centers are connected through redundant 10G WANs. The primary data center is 100% fiber to the rack. We independently manage our network using active-passive redundant BGP routers, with complete control over routing in both optimal and sub-optimal conditions.

Physical & Environmental Controls

Physical and environmental controls at our two mirrored data centers include:

  • 24x7 uniformed guard service
  • Electronic keycard access and biometric controls
  • Highly restricted facility access
  • Monitored alarms and CCTV
  • Redundant cooling systems
  • Fire detection and suppression systems
  • High sensitivity, early warning smoke detection systems
  • Redundant power through uninterruptible power supply (UPS) systems and backup generators
  • Regular testing and maintenance
  • Regular SOC2 audits
